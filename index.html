Understanding Your BMI

What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a screening tool that measures your body weight relative to your height. It's calculated by dividing your weight in kilograms by the square of your height in meters (kg/m¬≤).

Why is BMI Important?

BMI helps identify whether you're at a healthy weight, which is important because:

  • Being underweight can lead to nutritional deficiencies and weakened immunity
  • Being overweight increases risk of heart disease, diabetes, and stroke
  • Maintaining a healthy weight reduces overall health risks
  • It's a quick screening tool for potential health issues

Limitations of BMI

While BMI is useful, it has limitations:

  • Doesn't distinguish between muscle and fat mass
  • Athletes may have high BMI due to muscle, not fat
  • Doesn't account for bone density or body composition
  • May not accurately reflect health for all ethnicities
  • Should be used alongside other health assessments

How to Improve Your BMI

  • Eat a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains
  • Exercise regularly - aim for 150 minutes of moderate activity per week
  • Stay hydrated and get adequate sleep
  • Reduce processed foods and sugary beverages
  • Consult healthcare professionals for personalized advice
